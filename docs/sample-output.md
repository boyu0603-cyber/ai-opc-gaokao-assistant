## Candidate Plan Categories

### Conservative

This category would include options that appear more stable relative to the student's profile and family preference. These options should prioritize admission safety, clearer fit, and lower uncertainty.

Advisor review focus:

- Check whether the options are too conservative.
- Confirm that the student would still be willing to attend these programs.
- Verify public information and current policy context.

### Balanced

This category would include options that match the student's interests while keeping risk at a manageable level. These choices should balance school profile, major fit, location, and family expectations.

Advisor review focus:

- Confirm whether the trade-off between major and city is acceptable.
- Compare several program types before narrowing the list.
- Explain why these options form the core of the plan.

### Ambitious

This category would include a small number of higher-upside options. These should be presented with explicit uncertainty and should not dominate the final plan.

Advisor review focus:

- Avoid overconfidence.
- Make the risk clear to the family.
- Confirm whether the student wants to include these options despite uncertainty.

## Example Explanation

Based on the synthetic profile, a balanced strategy may be more suitable than an aggressive strategy. The student has several interests and the family values employment stability, so the planning process should not focus only on prestige. A better approach is to compare programs by fit, city, future pathway, and risk category.

The conservative category protects against excessive uncertainty. The balanced category provides the main planning direction. The ambitious category may be included only if the family understands that these options carry higher uncertainty and require advisor review.

## Example Risk Notes

- Risk category labels are advisory and should not be treated as guarantees.
- Public information must be checked for recency and relevance.
- The student's final preference ranking may change after reviewing program details.
- Any plan should be reviewed by a human advisor before discussion with the family.

## Human Advisor Review Notes

The advisor should confirm:

- Whether the synthetic profile has enough information to draft a real plan.
- Whether the student has a stronger preference for major, city, or institution type.
- Whether any family constraints are missing.
- Whether source references are current.
- Whether risk explanations are understandable and not overstated.

## Revised Summary

After advisor review, the summary should be revised to:

- Remove unsupported assumptions.
- Add missing constraints.
- Clarify the student's priority order.
- Rebalance conservative, balanced, and ambitious categories.
- Include open questions for the next consultation.

## Advisor-ready Output Format

The intended final report structure is:

- Student Profile Summary
- Key Constraints
- Preference Summary
- Candidate Plan Categories
- Risk Explanation
- Open Questions
- Advisor Notes
- Next Consultation Actions

This example is intentionally generic and synthetic. It is included to show report structure, not to provide real admission advice.
