# Fit with SUTD MSc DAI-E

## Why This Project Fits

The AI OPC Gaokao Assistant fits the SUTD MSc in Design and Artificial Intelligence for Enterprise because it sits at the intersection of design-led problem framing, AI-enabled workflow building, and enterprise-style decision support.

The project is not only about generating answers with a language model. It is about designing a responsible process for a real advisory setting where information is incomplete, decisions are high-stakes, and human review remains necessary.

## Design

The project begins with a human decision journey: how students and families move from anxiety and fragmented information to a structured planning conversation.

Design work includes:

- Understanding user pain points.
- Structuring the consultation journey.
- Turning unclear preferences into reviewable planning inputs.
- Making explanations understandable for non-technical users.
- Designing outputs that support discussion rather than blind acceptance.

## AI

AI is used as a workflow component rather than a final authority.

Relevant AI functions include:

- Information structuring.
- Clarification question generation.
- Preference and constraint summarization.
- Retrieval-assisted reasoning.
- Draft explanation generation.
- Report preparation for human review.

This aligns with practical AI system building, where the value comes from combining model capability with process design and domain context.

## Enterprise

The project reflects enterprise AI concerns because it requires repeatable workflow, role separation, review checkpoints, data governance, and output quality control.

Enterprise-relevant elements include:

- Intake standardization.
- Modular workflow architecture.
- Advisor review loop.
- Source provenance.
- Evaluation rubric.
- Privacy boundary.
- Future dashboard and pilot consultation workflow.

## Human-AI Interaction

The system is designed around cooperation between AI and human advisors. AI prepares structured drafts and explanations. Human advisors validate, revise, and contextualize the output before family discussion.

This interaction model is important because the final decision depends on values, uncertainty, and personal constraints that cannot be reduced to a single automated answer.

## Responsible AI

Responsible AI boundaries are central to the project.

The repository makes clear that:

- AI outputs require human review.
- Real student data is excluded.
- Synthetic examples are used for public documentation.
- Source provenance and uncertainty disclosure are necessary.
- The system is an early-stage portfolio case, not a fully production-ready product.
