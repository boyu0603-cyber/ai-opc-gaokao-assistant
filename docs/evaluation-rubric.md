# Evaluation Rubric

## Purpose

This rubric defines how AI-assisted planning outputs should be reviewed before they are used in consultation. The goal is to evaluate usefulness, clarity, safety, and readiness for human advisor discussion.

| Evaluation Area | What Good Looks Like | Review Questions | Risk if Weak |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Factual grounding | Claims are connected to public or advisor-approved sources, and uncertain information is marked. | Are key claims source-backed? Is the source current and relevant? | The report may sound confident while relying on unsupported or outdated information. |
| Preference alignment | The output reflects the student's stated priorities and family constraints. | Does the plan match major, location, risk, and family preferences? | The plan may be technically plausible but personally unsuitable. |
| Risk clarity | Conservative, balanced, and ambitious categories are explained in plain language. | Are risk categories understandable? Are guarantees avoided? | Families may misunderstand uncertainty or overtrust the draft. |
| Explainability | The reasoning is transparent enough for advisor and family review. | Can the advisor explain why an option appears in a category? | Recommendations may feel like a black box. |
| Completeness | The output covers profile assumptions, constraints, candidate categories, trade-offs, and open questions. | What important planning input is missing? | The report may skip information needed for a responsible decision. |
| Human review readiness | The draft is organized for advisor checking and revision. | Can an advisor quickly see what to verify or change? | The output may be difficult to review before consultation. |
| Uncertainty disclosure | Limitations, missing data, and assumptions are clearly stated. | Are uncertain points visible? Are assumptions labeled? | The report may overstate confidence. |
| Safety and privacy compliance | No private data, credentials, private prompts, or real cases are exposed. | Does the output contain only approved or synthetic information? | The repository or consultation process may create privacy risk. |

## Review Outcome Levels

| Level | Meaning | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ready for advisor discussion | The draft is clear, grounded, and safe enough for advisor review. | Advisor reviews and revises before family discussion. |
| Needs revision | Some assumptions, sources, or explanations require improvement. | Return to clarification, source checking, or explanation rewrite. |
| Not suitable | The output contains unsupported claims, privacy risk, or confusing reasoning. | Do not use. Redraft from structured inputs. |

## Notes

The rubric is intended for quality control, not automated scoring. It supports responsible AI workflow design by making review criteria explicit.
